## TYPE 1-5: TIMEWORK BASICS (Q141-Q165)

**Q141** A completes job in 12 days. Work done in 5 days?
- A) Rate = 1/12 per day
- B) Work in 5 days = 5/12 of job
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Easy

**Q142** A completes in 8 days, B in 12 days. Together?
- A) A's rate 1/8, B's rate 1/12
- B) Combined = 1/8 + 1/12 = 5/24 per day
- C) Time = 24/5 = 4.8 days
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Easy

**Q143** Two workers: A (6d), B (12d) together. Days to complete?
- A) Rate: 1/6 + 1/12 = 1/4
- B) Time = 4 days
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Easy

**Q144** A and B together 10 days. A alone 15 days. B alone?
- A) Combined 1/10, A = 1/15
- B) B = 1/10 - 1/15 = 1/30
- C) B alone 30 days
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Medium

**Q145** Three workers A(6d), B(8d), C(12d) together?
- A) Combined rate 1/6 + 1/8 + 1/12 = 9/24 = 3/8
- B) Time = 8/3 ≈ 2.67 days
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Medium

## TYPE 6-10: COMPLEX TIMEWORK (Q151-Q165)

**Q151** A is 50% more efficient than B. Together 20 days. A alone?
- A) B's rate = x, A's rate = 1.5x
- B) 2.5x = 1/20 → x = 1/50
- C) A's rate = 3/100, A alone = 100/3 ≈ 33.3 days
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Hard

**Q152** Job split: A works 4 days then B continues 6 days. A alone 12d, B alone?
- A) A's work = 4/12 = 1/3
- B) B's work = 2/3, at rate 1/B, so 6/(B) = 2/3 → B = 9 days
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Hard

**Q153** 10 workers, 20 days project. After 5 days, 2 workers leave. Extra days needed?
- A) Work in 5 days = 50 worker-days done
- B) Remaining = 200 - 50 = 150 worker-days
- C) With 8 workers = 150/8 = 18.75 days extra
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Hard

**Q154** A and B together complete 2/3 job in 10 days. C joins, all three complete remaining 1/3 in 3 days. C's rate?
- A) A+B rate = (2/3)/10 = 1/15 per day
- B) A+B+C rate = (1/3)/3 = 1/9 per day
- C) C rate = 1/9 - 1/15 = 2/45 per day
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Expert

**Q155** Interview question: Project scheduling. 50 worker-days work needed. How allocate 5 workers over how many days?
- A) Total = 50 worker-days
- B) Days needed = 50/5 = 10 days
- C) Or 2 workers for 25 days, or 10 workers for 5 days
- D) All above show flexibility | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Expert

## TYPE 7-10: PIPES & INTRODUCTION (Q156-Q165)

**Q156** Pipe A fills tank in 6 hours. Rate?
- A) 1/6 tank per hour
- B) In 4 hours fill 4/6 = 2/3 tank
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Easy

**Q157** Two pipes A (fills 6h), B (fills 8h) together?
- A) Combined 1/6 + 1/8 = 7/24 per hour
- B) Time = 24/7 ≈ 3.43 hours
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Easy

**Q158** Pipe A fills, drain empties tank. A fills in 4 hours, drain empties in 6 hours. Net?
- A) Net rate = 1/4 - 1/6 = 1/12 per hour
- B) Time to fill = 12 hours
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Medium

**Q159** Three pipes A (1/6 per hour), B (1/8 per hour), C (drain, 1/10 per hour). All open?
- A) Net = 1/6 + 1/8 - 1/10 = 20/120 + 15/120 - 12/120 = 23/120
- B) Time = 120/23 ≈ 5.2 hours
- C) Both A and B
- D) All above | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Hard

**Q160** Interview: Combination understanding. A and B pipes analogy to workers. How similar?
- A) Both rates measured per unit time
- B) Combined rates sum: 1/6 + 1/8 = 7/24
- C) Work model applies: worker-days = pipe-hours analogy
- D) All show deep understanding | **Answer:** D | **Difficulty:** Expert
