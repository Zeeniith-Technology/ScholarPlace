## TITLE DAY 3 WEDNESDAY PIPES FUNDAMENTALS - PART 2 DEEP CONCEPT: PIPE RATES

**Fundamental Formula:**

If a pipe fills a tank in **T minutes**, then:
- **Rate = 1/T tank per minute**

**Example:**
- Pipe A fills tank in 12 minutes ‚Üí Rate_A = 1/12 tank/min
- Pipe B fills tank in 15 minutes ‚Üí Rate_B = 1/15 tank/min

**Combined Rate (Both Open Simultaneously):**
- Combined = Rate_A + Rate_B = 1/12 + 1/15
- LCM(12, 15) = 60
- Combined = 5/60 + 4/60 = **9/60 = 3/20 tank/min**
- Time to fill = 1 √∑ (3/20) = **20/3 ‚âà 6.67 minutes**

**Verification:**
- In 6.67 minutes:
  - A fills: 6.67 √ó (1/12) = 0.556 tank
  - B fills: 6.67 √ó (1/15) = 0.444 tank
  - Total: 0.556 + 0.444 = 1.0 tank ‚úì

**Q:** A pipe fills a 300-liter tank in 6 minutes. What is the pipe's filling rate? How long to fill a 450-liter tank?

**Ex 3.8: Finding Individual Rates from Combined**

- C = (A + B + C) - (A + B) = 3/16 - 1/6
  - LCM(16, 6) = 48
  - C = 9/48 - 8/48 = **1/48 tank/min**
  - Time_C = **48 minutes**

**Verification:**
- A + B = 5/48 + 3/48 = 8/48 = 1/6 ‚úì
- A + C = 5/48 + 1/48 = 6/48 = 1/8 ‚úì
- B + C = 3/48 + 1/48 = 4/48 = 1/12 ‚úì

## TITLE DAY 3 WEDNESDAY PIPES FUNDAMENTALS - PART 4 COMMON MISTAKES DAY 3

**Mistake 1: Subtracting Times Instead of Adding Rates**

‚ùå **Error:** "A fills in 10m, B fills in 15m. Together = 10 - 15 = -5 minutes?"

‚úÖ **Reality:** Rates add, times don't subtract. Combined rate = 1/10 + 1/15, then invert.

**Prevention:** Always work with rates (fractions), never subtract times directly.

---

**Mistake 2: Confusing Volume with Rate**

‚ùå **Error:** "Pipe fills 300L in 6 min. Rate = 300/6 = 50. So time for 450L = 450/50 = 9 min?" (This is actually correct, but student might confuse units)

‚úÖ **Reality:** Rate = volume/time. For fractional approach: Rate = 1/tank per time.

**Prevention:** Clarify whether using absolute volumes or fractional tank approach.

---

**Mistake 3: Not Accounting for Partial Filling**

‚ùå **Error:** "Tank 3/4 full. Pipe fills in 8 min. Time to full = 8 min?"

‚úÖ **Reality:** Only 1/4 remaining. Time = (1/4) √∑ (1/8) = 2 minutes.

**Prevention:** Always calculate remaining work first, then apply rate.

## TITLE DAY 4 THURSDAY PIPES ADVANCED - PART 1 LEAKS & NEGATIVE RATES (DEEP)

**Critical Concept:** Leak = negative rate (removes water from tank)

**Fundamental Principle:**
- Inlet pipe: Rate = +1/T (fills tank)
- Leak/outlet: Rate = -1/T (empties tank)
- **Net rate = Inlet rate - Leak rate**

**Key Insight:** If net rate > 0, tank fills. If net rate < 0, tank empties!

---

**Ex 4.1: Single Inlet + Single Leak (Basic)**

**Q:** Pipe A fills tank in 10 minutes. There's a leak that empties full tank in 20 minutes. Both active (pipe filling, leak draining). Net result? Time to fill?

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Individual Rates**
- Inlet A: Rate = +1/10 tank/min (positive = fills)
- Leak: Rate = -1/20 tank/min (negative = empties)

**Step 2: Net Rate**
- Net = 1/10 - 1/20
- Net = 2/20 - 1/20 = **1/20 tank/min**

**Step 3: Time to Fill**
- Time = 1 √∑ (1/20) = **20 minutes**

**Interpretation:** Leak slows filling. Without leak: 10 minutes. With leak: 20 minutes (twice as long).

**Verification:**
- In 20 minutes:
  - Inlet fills: 20 √ó (1/10) = 2.0 tanks
  - Leak empties: 20 √ó (1/20) = 1.0 tank
  - Net: 2.0 - 1.0 = 1.0 tank ‚úì

---

**Ex 4.2: Leak Faster Than Inlet (Tank Empties!)**

**Q:** Inlet fills tank in 8 minutes. Leak empties tank in 6 minutes. Both active. What happens?

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Rates**
- Inlet: +1/8 tank/min
- Leak: -1/6 tank/min

**Step 2: Net Rate**
- Net = 1/8 - 1/6
- LCM(8, 6) = 24
- Net = 3/24 - 4/24 = **-1/24 tank/min** (NEGATIVE!)

**Step 3: Interpretation**
- Negative net rate means tank **EMPTIES**, not fills!
- Time to empty full tank = 1 √∑ (1/24) = **24 minutes**

**Critical Insight:** When leak rate > inlet rate, tank drains even with inlet open!

**Verification:**
- Starting with full tank (1.0)
- In 24 minutes:
  - Inlet adds: 24 √ó (1/8) = 3.0 tanks
  - Leak removes: 24 √ó (1/6) = 4.0 tanks
  - Net: 3.0 - 4.0 = -1.0 (tank empties) ‚úì

---

**Ex 4.5: Simple Alternate Pattern**

**Q:** Pipe A (fills in 8 min) works for 3 minutes, then Pipe B (fills in 12 min) works for 4 minutes. Pattern repeats. When is tank full?

- Cycle time = 3 + 4 = **7 minutes**

- A's work: 3/8 = 9/24 tank

- Full tank = 24/24
- Cycles needed = 24/24 √∑ 17/24 = 24/17 ‚âà **1.41 cycles**

**Step 3: Calculate Time**
- Full cycle 1: 7 minutes, fills 17/24
- Remaining: 24/24 - 17/24 = 7/24 tank
- Next cycle: A works 3 min, fills 3/8 = 9/24 (more than needed!)
- Time in second cycle: (7/24) √∑ (1/8) = (7/24) √ó 8 = 56/24 = 7/3 ‚âà 2.33 minutes

**Total Time:**
- Total = 7 + 2.33 = **9.33 minutes**

**Verification:**
- Cycle 1 (7m): 17/24 tank
- Cycle 2 partial (2.33m of A): 2.33 √ó (1/8) = 2.33/8 = 0.291 = 7/24 tank
- Total: 17/24 + 7/24 = 24/24 = 1 tank ‚úì

## TITLE DAY 4 THURSDAY PIPES ADVANCED - PART 3 CISTERNS (PERMANENT OUTLET)

**Concept:** Cistern = tank with permanent outlet (always open, unlike leak that can be closed)

**Key Formula:**
If inlet fills in **F** minutes alone, outlet empties in **E** minutes alone:
- **Net rate = 1/F - 1/E**
- **Time to fill = 1 √∑ (Net rate) = F √ó E / (E - F)** (when E > F, i.e., outlet slower)

**Critical:** Formula only works when inlet is faster than outlet (F < E in time, meaning 1/F > 1/E in rate).

---

**Ex 4.7: Basic Cistern**

**Q:** Inlet fills cistern in 20 minutes. Outlet empties in 30 minutes. Both open. Time to fill empty cistern?

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Rates**
- Inlet: +1/20 tank/min
- Outlet: -1/30 tank/min

**Step 2: Net Rate**
- Net = 1/20 - 1/30
- LCM(20, 30) = 60
- Net = 3/60 - 2/60 = **1/60 tank/min**

**Step 3: Time to Fill**
- Time = 1 √∑ (1/60) = **60 minutes**

**Using Formula:**
- Time = (20 √ó 30) / (30 - 20) = 600 / 10 = **60 minutes** ‚úì

**Verification:**
- In 60 minutes:
  - Inlet adds: 60 √ó (1/20) = 3.0 tanks
  - Outlet removes: 60 √ó (1/30) = 2.0 tanks
  - Net: 3.0 - 2.0 = 1.0 tank ‚úì

---

**Ex 4.8: Outlet Faster Than Inlet (Cistern Empties)**

**Q:** Inlet fills in 30 minutes. Outlet empties in 20 minutes. Both open. What happens?

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Rates**
- Inlet: +1/30 tank/min
- Outlet: -1/20 tank/min

**Step 2: Net Rate**
- Net = 1/30 - 1/20
- Net = 2/60 - 3/60 = **-1/60 tank/min** (NEGATIVE!)

**Step 3: Interpretation**
- Negative net = cistern **EMPTIES**
- Time to empty full cistern = 1 √∑ (1/60) = **60 minutes**

**Critical:** When outlet is faster, cistern drains even with inlet open!
