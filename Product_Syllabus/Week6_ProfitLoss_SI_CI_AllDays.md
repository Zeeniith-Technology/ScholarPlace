## TITLE DAY 1 MONDAY PROFIT & LOSS FUNDAMENTALS - PART 2 DEEP CONCEPT: PROFIT AS A RATE (WEEK 5 TRANSFER)

**Fundamental Definitions:**

| Term | Definition | Formula |
|------|------------|---------|
| **CP** | Cost Price | Amount merchant pays to acquire good |
| **SP** | Selling Price | Amount merchant receives when selling |
| **Profit** | SP > CP | SP - CP |
| **Loss** | SP < CP | CP - SP |
| **Profit %** | Profit relative to CP | **(SP-CP)/CP √ó 100%** |
| **Loss %** | Loss relative to CP | **(CP-SP)/CP √ó 100%** |

**Mathematical Identity (Week 5 Parallel):**

Profit% = (SP - CP) / CP √ó 100% = **(SP/CP - 1) √ó 100%**

This is **exactly** like:
- Week 5: Speed = Distance/Time
- Here: SP/CP = 1 + Profit%/100 (multiplicative rate!)

**Example:**
- CP = 100, SP = 120 ‚Üí Profit = 20, Profit% = 20/100 √ó 100% = **20%**
- SP/CP = 120/100 = 1.2 = 1 + 20/100 ‚úì

---

**SP Formula:**
$$\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \left(1 + \frac{\text{Profit\%}}{100}\right)$$

**CP Formula:**
$$\text{CP} = \frac{\text{SP}}{1 + \frac{\text{Profit\%}}{100}}$$

**Loss Formula (Negative Profit):**
$$\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \left(1 - \frac{\text{Loss\%}}{100}\right)$$

**Ex 1.6: When Does Loss% Equal Profit%?**

Q: What CP and SP relationship makes loss% numerically equal to profit%?

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Set up equation**
- Let loss% = profit% = x
- For loss: Loss = CP √ó (x/100)
- For profit: Profit = CP √ó (x/100)

**Step 2: Conditions**
- Loss scenario: SP = CP - CP(x/100) = CP(1 - x/100)
- Profit scenario: SP = CP + CP(x/100) = CP(1 + x/100)

**Step 3: Insight**
- Loss% and profit% are numerically equal only in magnitude
- They represent different relationships:
  - Loss: percentage of original CP lost
  - Profit: percentage of original CP gained

**Step 3: Profit %**
- Profit% = ((1/3)CP / CP) √ó 100% = (1/3) √ó 100% = **33.33%**

**Example:**

## TITLE DAY 2 TUESDAY ADVANCED P&L - PART 4 DISHONEST DEALING: FAKE WEIGHTS

**Concept - Merchant Uses False Weights:**

A dishonest merchant might:
- **Claim:** Selling 1 kg at price X
- **Reality:** Using 900g weight, actually giving only 900g
- **Profit:** Sells 900g but customer pays for 1000g!

**Formula for Profit% (Fake Weights):**

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{Claimed Weight} - \text{Actual Weight}}{\text{Actual Weight}} \times 100\%$$

**Example:**
- Claims 1000g, gives 800g
- Profit% = ((1000-800)/800) √ó 100 = (200/800) √ó 100 = **25%**

---

**Ex 2.6: Dishonest Dealing - Buying Fake Weights**

- Cost: 120 (paid for 1.2kg but got 1kg of goods) = 120 per kg

- **Profit% = (125-120)/120 √ó 100 = 4.17%**

**Day 3 Mission - SIMPLE INTEREST (SI):**

Interest = "Cost of borrowing money" or "Return on investment"

**Three Perspectives:**
1. **Borrower:** Pays interest for borrowed money
2. **Lender:** Receives interest for lending money
3. **Investor:** Earns interest on invested capital

**Critical Formula (Simple Interest):**

$$\text{SI} = \frac{\text{Principal} \times \text{Rate} \times \text{Time}}{100}$$

Where:
- **Principal (P):** Initial amount borrowed/invested
- **Rate (R):** Annual interest rate (%)
- **Time (T):** Duration in years
- **SI:** Simple Interest earned/paid

**Amount (Total Money After Interest):**

$$\text{Amount (A)} = \text{Principal} + \text{SI} = P + \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$$

$$\text{A} = P \left(1 + \frac{R \times T}{100}\right)$$
